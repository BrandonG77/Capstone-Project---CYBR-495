## Detection Alert

The Detection Engineer created detection rules to identify potential phishing attacks on a web-based application. Meant to detect when credentials are entered into fraudulent submission boxes on a fake website, these rules can be used to automatically detect phishing attacks and create an incident ticket if it's triggered. The Splunk SPL queries created allows an organization to see live web traffic as it occurs on a company network, which can be used to shut down any further phishing attacks before they happen.

Some of the most common responses for a phishing attack include

- Implementing multi-factor authentication across all used platforms
- Verifying the source of an email or other message and considering whether its context seems legitimate
- Using password managers to store credentials rather than autofilling passwords every time
- Updating software regularly
- Disconnecting a victimized device from the internet immediately
- Change the account's credentials if they've been compromised
- Use browser extensions to warm you of malicious websites before visiting them

Many, all, or none of these response methods will be implemented depending on the nature of the threat.

## Investigation

A company's employees and customers often receive many emails everyday. For some, it's tempting to automatically click links or provide information when the message is from someone they recognize. However, phishing attacks use inconsistent tactics to persuade users that a message is legitimate when in reality it's used to gather sensitive data.

Phishing comes in all shapes and sizes, from emails to text messages to look-alike websites. Email phishing is one of the most common methods, as it's generally a company's primary form of communication. However, attackers can create an identical imitating website that fools a user into entering sensitive data. As a result, attackers can use this information to gain access to the victim's account and damage the organization.

## Containment

Since this phishing attack simulation used an identical website to one a company uses, it's easy for a user to mistakenly enter their username, password, financial data, or other confidential information into the website without realizing it's being stolen.

Training all parties associated with a company--employees, clients, investors, and others--ensures they know how to properly identify a phishing attempt before it's too late. Following strict MFA and credential storage guidelines can protect someone's login information from ever being discovered. Likewise, a variety of cybersecurity training resources can be provided to these individuals to increase awareness of the effects even one single phishing attack can cause.

Using one or several of these risk reduction tactics ensures users don't fall victim to phishing attacks to begin with. This protects the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the organization's data.
